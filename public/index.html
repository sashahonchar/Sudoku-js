Cracking The Cryptic Web App Alpha Test

Introduction

Hi,

I'm Sven (killroy on Discord), and I am working on a new version of the Cracking The Cryptic web app. Our aim is to replace the current app with an equivalent tool in the next month or so, which will prepare us for adding all those features we've been planning.

As a valued contributor to the CTC community, and experienced puzzler, I would appreciate your help in testing the new app to ensure it will work as well as possible.

The plan is to spend around 2 weeks on testing amongst us, to find the most pressing issues, before handing it over to Simon and Mark to ensure the tool works for the video production workflow.

The testing will proceed in two phases of around 1-3 weeks each:

Phase 1

  1. Test core functionality and find the worst and most obvious issues
  2. Test support for a wide variety of existing puzzles
  3. Test mouse and keyboard controls
  4. Test for any performance issues
  5. Fine-tune usability and UX behaviours
  6. Ignore currently known visual issues (such as ordering of overlays, etc)
  7. Ignore scaling issues on mobile platforms
  8. Ignore any issues that are also present in the legacy app

Phase 2

  1. Fine-tune visual details
  2. Test layout and resizing on various displays and mobile platforms
  3. Test extreme test puzzles for edge cases
  4. Test several new features, such as dark mode and an improved checker function
  5. Collect suggestions for improvements, worthwhile changes and updates, and requests for completely new features

Currently I am cleaning up some remaining issues (clearing cells, some selection issues, etc), and I will create a selection of test puzzles for phase one, as well as some UI elements to navigate these.

If you are interested in helping out, just post in the test channel #webapp-testing on discord, or, if you do not have access, let me or one of the mods know.

I truly appreciate your help, and hope the new app will be a welcome improvement.

Kind Regards,

Sven

Araf Sudoku by Phistomefel

Hosted by Simon
Play Araf Sudoku
View YouTube Video

Rules

Normal sudoku rules apply. The grid must be decomposed into different areas. Each cell belongs to exactly one area. Each area contains exactly two clues. The sum of all digits in an area lies between the two clues, but may not reach them. For example, if the clues for an area are 21 and 24, the sum of the digits in the area is 22 or 23. Digits may not repeat within an area.

16 Cups Of Tea by Sumanta Mukherjee

Hosted by Simon
Play 16 Cups Of Tea
View YouTube Video

Rules

Normal sudoku rules apply. In cages, digits must sum to the small clue given in the top left corner of the cage. Digits cannot repeat in a cage. Clues outside the grid give the sum of cells along the indicated diagonal. Inequality signs in the grid point to the lower of the two cells involved.

Battlefield Sudoku by Big Tiger

Hosted by Simon
Play Battlefield Sudoku
View YouTube Video

Rules

Normal sudoku rules apply. Consider the first X cells and the last Y cells of a row or column where X is the number in the first cell and Y is the number in the last cell. A clue outside the grid gives the sum of the digits where these groups overlap, or the sum of the digits in the gap between the groups if they don't overlap.
